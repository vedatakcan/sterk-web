Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 26, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Sterk ("we," "our," or "App") handles user data. Your privacy is our priority, and we operate with a strict data minimization principle.

1. Personal Data and Identity

Sterk does not collect any personal information that can directly identify you, such as your name, email address, phone number, or physical location. We do not require any user account or login to play the game.

  • Local Data: Your game progress, unlocked levels, and high scores are stored exclusively on your device.

2. Data Collection (Third-Party Services)

To provide a free experience and improve app stability, we work with third-party partners like Google AdMob. These partners may collect the following data types:

  • Device Identifiers: We may use device-specific IDs (such as IDFA for iOS) for advertising and analytics purposes.
  • Product Interaction: We monitor how users interact with the app (e.g., levels completed, time spent) to improve game balance and features.
  • Advertising Data: We collect data regarding the performance of ads shown within the app (e.g., ad views and clicks).
  • Diagnostics (Crash Data): We collect technical logs to identify and fix bugs or crashes to ensure a smooth gameplay experience.

3. Tracking and Linking Data

While third-party services use identifiers to serve relevant ads and perform analytics, none of this data is linked to your real-world identity.

We use these identifiers for tracking purposes as defined by Apple’s transparency guidelines to show you more relevant advertisements and measure their effectiveness.

4. Data Security

We take all necessary technical measures to protect the integrity of the data stored locally on your device. Please note that third-party services (like Google AdMob) manage data according to their own secure privacy standards.

5. Children's Privacy

Sterk is a word puzzle game suitable for all ages. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. Our advertising partners are instructed to comply with child safety regulations (such as COPPA and GDPR).
